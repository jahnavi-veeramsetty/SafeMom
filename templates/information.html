Pregnancy Risk Information

Here you can access vital information about pregnancy risks and how to monitor your health.

Common Pregnancy Risk Factors

  • Age: Women over 35 or under 18 may face higher risks during pregnancy.
  • Blood Pressure: High blood pressure can lead to complications like preeclampsia.
  • Blood Sugar: Gestational diabetes affects both mother and baby, leading to long-term health risks.
  • Lifestyle Choices: Smoking, alcohol consumption, and poor diet can significantly impact pregnancy outcomes.

Tips for a Healthy Pregnancy

  • Maintain a balanced diet
  • Get regular exercise (as recommended by your doctor)
  • Stay hydrated and manage stress
  • Get adequate prenatal care and follow your doctor's advice
